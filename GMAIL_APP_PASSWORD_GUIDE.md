# Gmail App Password Setup Guide

## Why You Need This
Your website needs to send email notifications when someone submits the onboarding form. Gmail requires a special "App Password" for this to work securely.

## Error You're Seeing

## Steps to Generate a New App Password

### Step 1: Enable 2-Step Verification (if not already enabled)
1. Go to: https://myaccount.google.com/security
2. Sign in with your Gmail account (`jerryfrancis465@gmail.com`)
3. Under "How you sign in to Google", click **2-Step Verification**
4. Follow the prompts to enable it (if not already enabled)

### Step 2: Generate App Password
1. Go directly to: https://myaccount.google.com/apppasswords
2. Sign in if prompted
3. You'll see "App passwords" page
4. Click **Select app** → Choose **Mail**
5. Click **Select device** → Choose **Windows Computer** (or Other)
6. Click **Generate**

### Step 3: Copy Your New Password
- You'll see a 16-character password like: `abcd efgh ijkl mnop`
- **Copy this password** (you can include or remove spaces)
- **Important:** You won't be able to see this password again!

## Troubleshooting

### "App passwords" option not showing?
- Make sure 2-Step Verification is **enabled** first
- Use this direct link: https://myaccount.google.com/apppasswords

### Using a Google Workspace account?
- Your admin may need to allow "Less secure app access" or enable App Passwords
- Contact your Google Workspace administrator

### Want to use a different email?
If you prefer to use a different Gmail account (like `support@marketlyn.com` if it's a Gmail/Workspace account), just provide:
1. The Gmail address
2. The App Password for that account

---

## Security Note
- App Passwords are designed for this purpose and are safe to use
- They can be revoked anytime from your Google Account settings
- Never share your main Gmail password - only the App Password
